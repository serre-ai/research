# Research Idea Grading Rubric

Scoring system for evaluating research idea candidates. Used by agents and humans to assess ideas generated by the weekly synthesis pipeline.

## Composite Score (0–100)

| Dimension | Max Points | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Novelty | 25 | How new is the core claim? |
| Feasibility | 20 | Can we execute this with current resources? |
| Impact | 15 | How much does this advance the field? |
| Venue Fit | 15 | Does this match a target venue's scope? |
| Portfolio Fit | 15 | Does this complement existing projects? |
| Risk | 10 | How well-bounded are the unknowns? |

**Composite = novelty + feasibility + impact + venue_fit + portfolio_fit + risk**

## Dimension Rubrics

### Novelty (0–25)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 21–25 | No prior work addresses this specific question. New framing, new connection, or new formalization of an open problem. |
| 16–20 | Prior work exists but misses a key angle. Our approach introduces a meaningful twist (different formalism, unexplored variable, cross-domain transfer). |
| 11–15 | Incremental advance on existing work. Same question, better method or more data. Publishable but not exciting. |
| 6–10 | Marginal novelty. Similar work exists with minor differences. Needs strong execution to stand out. |
| 0–5 | Already done. Concurrent or prior work covers the same ground. |

### Feasibility (0–20)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 17–20 | Can be completed with existing infrastructure, data, and budget. Clear methodology with known tools. Estimated effort ≤4 weeks. |
| 13–16 | Achievable but requires some new infrastructure or data collection. Methodology is sound but untested at our scale. 4–8 weeks. |
| 9–12 | Significant unknowns. Requires new tooling, large compute, or access to external resources. 8–12 weeks. |
| 5–8 | High uncertainty. Key steps depend on unproven methods or unavailable resources. >12 weeks. |
| 0–4 | Infeasible with current resources. Requires capabilities we don't have. |

### Impact (0–15)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 13–15 | Changes how the field thinks about the problem. Introduces a framework, benchmark, or result that others will build on. |
| 10–12 | Strong contribution. Resolves an open question, provides missing evidence, or enables new research directions. |
| 7–9 | Solid contribution. Useful result that advances understanding but doesn't reshape the field. |
| 4–6 | Minor contribution. Confirms what was suspected or provides a data point without broader implications. |
| 0–3 | Negligible impact. Unlikely to be cited or influence future work. |

### Venue Fit (0–15)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 13–15 | Perfect match. The venue's top-3 topics include our area. Recent accepted papers address similar questions. Deadline is reachable. |
| 10–12 | Good fit. Our topic is within scope. Some recent papers in the area. Deadline requires focused effort. |
| 7–9 | Moderate fit. The venue covers our area but it's not a primary focus. Might need to frame carefully. |
| 4–6 | Weak fit. Our work is at the edges of the venue's scope. Risk of desk rejection. |
| 0–3 | Poor fit. Wrong venue for this type of work. |

### Portfolio Fit (0–15)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 13–15 | Directly extends or complements an active project. Shares data, methodology, or theoretical framework. Synergies are concrete. |
| 10–12 | Related to active projects. Builds on findings or methods we've developed. Some shared infrastructure. |
| 7–9 | Thematically adjacent. Same broad area but limited concrete synergies. |
| 4–6 | New direction with weak ties to existing work. Diversifies the portfolio but doesn't leverage current assets. |
| 0–3 | Completely orthogonal. No connection to ongoing work. Would dilute focus. |

### Risk (0–10)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Well-bounded. Key risks are identified and have mitigations. Failure modes are recoverable (partial results are still publishable). |
| 7–8 | Manageable risk. 1–2 unknowns that could delay but not kill the project. Contingency plans exist. |
| 5–6 | Moderate risk. Success depends on empirical results going a particular way. No clear fallback. |
| 3–4 | High risk. Multiple critical unknowns. Project could fail entirely with nothing publishable. |
| 0–2 | Extreme risk. Success requires multiple unlikely things to work simultaneously. |

## Thresholds

| Composite Score | Action |
|----------------|--------|
| ≥75 | **Auto-approve**: Move to `ideas/drafts/`, create Linear issue, schedule scoping session. |
| 50–74 | **Human review**: Flag for review. Promising but needs refinement or strategic judgment. |
| 25–49 | **Park**: Keep in `ideas/candidates/` for future reconsideration. May improve as portfolio evolves. |
| <25 | **Reject**: Record reason in `rejected_reason` field. Do not revisit unless circumstances change. |

## Gap Type Bonuses

When an idea fills a specific gap detected by `scripts/gap-detector.py`, apply a bonus to the novelty score:

| Gap Type | Bonus | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Contradicting claims | +3 | Resolving contradictions is high-value and often overlooked. |
| Missing empirical | +2 | Theory without evidence is a clear publication opportunity. |
| Missing theory | +2 | Empirical results without explanation invite formal treatment. |
| Uncovered connection | +1 | Cross-pollination is novel but may be superficial. |
| New benchmark need | +1 | Benchmarks are needed but lower novelty ceiling. |

Bonuses are capped: novelty cannot exceed 25.
