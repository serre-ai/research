## 2. Page Limits

### At Submission
- **Main text:** 9 pages maximum (strictly enforced; exceeding triggers desk rejection)
- **References:** Unlimited (do not count toward page limit)
- **Appendix:** Unlimited pages after the bibliography (reviewers not required to read)
- **Reproducibility statement:** Does not count toward page limit
- **Ethics statement:** Does not count toward page limit (max 1 page recommended)
- **Acknowledgments:** Do not count toward page limit
- **LLM disclosure section:** Does not count toward page limit

### During Discussion/Rebuttal Phase
- **Main text increases to 10 pages** to accommodate revisions and new results

### Camera-Ready
- **Main text:** 10 pages maximum
- Same exclusions apply (references, appendix, statements, acknowledgments)

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide)

## 3. File Requirements

### Format
- **LaTeX** using the official ICLR 2026 style files (required)
- **PDF output** with US Letter paper size (not A4)
- Download template: https://github.com/ICLR/Master-Template/raw/master/iclr2026.zip

### Page Layout Specifications
- Text area: 5.5 inches wide x 9 inches long (33 x 54 picas)
- Left margin: 1.5 inches (9 picas)
- Top margin: 1 inch (6 picas)
- Body text: 10-point type, 11-point vertical spacing
- Typeface: Times New Roman
- Paragraphs separated by half-line space, no indentation
- Pages must be numbered (automatic with style file)

### Submission Upload
- Upload PDF via OpenReview
- Supplementary materials as a single additional file (see Section 12)
- No file size limits explicitly stated, but keep reasonable

### Style File Usage
- Do not modify any formatting parameters in the style files
- Use `\includegraphics` from the `graphicx` package for figures

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide), [ICLR Master Template](https://github.com/ICLR/Master-Template)

## 4. Desk-Rejection Triggers

Papers will be desk-rejected for any of the following:

1. **Author identity revealed** in main text or supplementary material
2. **Page limit exceeded** (over 9 pages of main text at submission)
3. **Placeholder or duplicate abstract** submitted
4. **No authors with OpenReview accounts** before the full paper deadline
5. **No qualified reviewer-registered author** -- at least one author must be registered to review a minimum of 3 papers (exceptions for new researchers)
6. **Undisclosed significant LLM usage** -- failure to disclose substantial LLM contribution to ideation or writing
7. **Dual submission detected** -- substantially similar paper published, accepted, or under review elsewhere
8. **Reviewer obligation failure** -- authors on 3+ papers who fail to complete reviews by rebuttal stage
9. **Hallucinated references or fabricated data** (with or without LLM involvement)
10. **Multiple similar paper variants** submitted without mutual acknowledgment (LLM-paraphrased spam)

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide), [ICLR Blog -- Response to LLM-Generated Papers](https://blog.iclr.cc/2025/11/19/iclr-2026-response-to-llm-generated-papers-and-reviews/)

## 5. Anonymization Rules

ICLR 2026 uses **double-blind review**: reviewers cannot see author names; authors cannot see reviewer names.

### Requirements
- **No author names or affiliations** anywhere in the submission (main text or supplementary)
- **No acknowledgments** that reveal identity during review (add them only at camera-ready)
- **Self-citations in third person:** "Smith et al. (2024) showed..." not "We previously showed (Smith et al., 2024)..."
- **Anonymous links only:** any URLs to code, demos, or websites must not reveal author identity
- **No visitor tracking** on any linked websites
- **Video demonstrations** must be completely anonymous

### What Does NOT Break Anonymization
- Having the same paper on arXiv (cite in third person)
- Appearing on challenge leaderboards (reviewers told not to search leaderboards)
- Workshop papers at non-archival venues

### During Discussion Phase
- Authors post comments anonymously
- Public commenters (non-authors/reviewers) must be non-anonymous
- Papers are deanonymized upon decision notification (January 22, 2026)

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide)

## 6. Style File Options

The ICLR 2026 LaTeX style file (`iclr2026_conference.sty`) supports three document options:

| Option | Usage | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| `\usepackage{iclr2026_conference}` | **Submission** (default) | Anonymous, line numbers enabled |
| `\usepackage[preprint]{iclr2026_conference}` | **Preprint** (arXiv posting) | Author names shown, no line numbers |
| `\usepackage[final]{iclr2026_conference}` | **Camera-ready** | Author names shown, final formatting |

### Template Files Included
- `iclr2026_conference.sty` -- main style file
- `iclr2026_conference.bst` -- BibTeX style file
- `iclr2026_conference.tex` -- example/template document
- `iclr2026_conference.bib` -- example bibliography
- `math_commands.tex` -- common math notation macros
- `fancyhdr.sty` -- header/footer formatting
- `natbib.sty` -- citation management

### Heading Styles
- **Title:** 17-point, small caps, left-aligned
- **Abstract heading:** 12-point, small caps, centered
- **First-level headings:** 12-point, small caps, flush left
- **Second-level headings:** 10-point, small caps, flush left
- **Third-level headings:** 10-point, small caps, flush left

### Abstract
- Indented 1/2 inch on both margins
- 10-point type, 11-point spacing
- Must be limited to one paragraph
- Preceded by two line spaces

**Source:** [ICLR Master Template](https://github.com/ICLR/Master-Template), [ICLR 2026 Formatting Instructions](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=qioDi3afqm)

## 8. Paper Checklist and Reproducibility

### Reproducibility Statement
- **Strongly encouraged** (not mandatory, but highly recommended)
- Place at end of main text, before references
- Approximately one paragraph long
- Does **not** count toward page limit
- Should reference: anonymous source code links, complete proofs in appendix, dataset details, hyperparameter settings, compute requirements, data processing pipelines

### What Reviewers Evaluate
Reviewers assess papers on four criteria (per the Reviewer Guide):

1. **Research problem clarity** -- Is the specific question well-defined?
2. **Motivation and literature placement** -- Is the approach well-motivated and properly situated?
3. **Evidence quality** -- Do theoretical arguments and empirical results support the claims?
4. **Significance and impact** -- Does it contribute new, relevant, impactful knowledge?

State-of-the-art results are explicitly **not required** for acceptance. Papers demonstrating new knowledge through empirical findings, theoretical advances, or practical contributions qualify.

### No NeurIPS-Style Checklist
Unlike NeurIPS, ICLR 2026 does **not** require a formal paper checklist with yes/no/N/A answers. Instead, ICLR relies on:
- The optional reproducibility statement
- The optional ethics statement
- The mandatory LLM disclosure (if applicable)
- The Code of Ethics acknowledgment in the submission form

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide), [ICLR 2026 Reviewer Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/ReviewerGuide)

## 9. LLM/Agent Usage Policy

ICLR 2026 has a detailed, strictly enforced LLM policy built on two core principles:

### Core Principles
1. **Mandatory disclosure:** Any use of an LLM must be disclosed -- all contributions to research must be acknowledged.
2. **Full accountability:** Authors and reviewers are ultimately responsible for all content, including LLM-generated output.

### Allowed Uses
- Grammar and wording improvements
- Section drafting assistance
- Research ideation support
- Experimental code generation
- Result analysis
- All of the above require **disclosure in the paper text and submission form**

### Prohibited Uses
- Using LLMs without disclosure (triggers desk rejection)
- Submitting unverified LLM output (hallucinated references, fabricated data)
- Hidden prompt injections (invisible text attempting to influence reviewers = collusion)
- Listing LLMs as authors (LLMs are ineligible for authorship)
- Submitting pre-publication papers to LLMs without considering confidentiality
- Submitting multiple LLM-paraphrased variants of the same paper

### Disclosure Requirements
- Describe the precise role of LLMs in the paper
- Place disclosure in a separate appendix section titled "LLM Usage" (or similar)
- Also indicate LLM usage in the OpenReview submission form
- This section does **not** count toward the page limit
- **Failure to disclose significant LLM usage results in desk rejection**

### Enforcement
- Detection tools used for triage, but action taken only on concrete evidence identified by ACs/SACs
- Consequences include desk rejection and potential banning from future submissions
- ICLR committed to publicly reporting violations

**Source:** [ICLR LLM Policy](https://iclr.cc/FAQ/LLM), [ICLR Blog -- LLM Policies](https://blog.iclr.cc/2025/08/26/policies-on-large-language-model-usage-at-iclr-2026/), [ICLR Blog -- Response to LLM-Generated Papers](https://blog.iclr.cc/2025/11/19/iclr-2026-response-to-llm-generated-papers-and-reviews/)

## 10. Dual Submission Policy

### Prohibited
- Submitting papers identical or substantially similar to versions previously published, accepted, or under review at any peer-reviewed venue (conference or journal)
- Policy is enforced throughout the entire review period

### Permitted
- Papers posted on **non-peer-reviewed** platforms (arXiv, bioRxiv, SSRN, etc.)
- Papers presented at **non-archival workshops** (including ICLR workshops)
- Papers that **cite related prior work** by the same authors (in third person)

### Key Clarification
The dual submission policy applies from the moment of submission through decision notification. If a paper is submitted to ICLR and concurrently submitted to or accepted at another peer-reviewed venue, it violates the policy and will be rejected.

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide), [ICLR 2026 Call for Papers](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/CallForPapers)

## 11. Preprint Policy

- **Posting to arXiv (or similar) is explicitly allowed** before, during, and after the review period
- Preprints do not violate the dual submission policy
- When citing your own arXiv preprint, use **third-person** language to maintain anonymity
- Use the `preprint` option in the style file for arXiv versions: `\usepackage[preprint]{iclr2026_conference}`
- ICLR provides anonymous BibTeX entries for papers under review, so you can cite concurrent ICLR submissions without breaking anonymity

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide)

## 12. Supplementary Materials Rules

### Format Options
ICLR encourages a **single PDF** containing both the main paper and supplementary text (appendix after references). Alternatively, supplementary materials can be uploaded as a separate file.

### Code Submission
Strongly encouraged for reproducibility. Three options:

1. **Anonymized .zip file** uploaded as supplementary material on OpenReview
2. **Anonymous repository link** included in the paper (e.g., Anonymous GitHub)
3. **Post link during discussion phase** visible only to reviewers and ACs (not public)

### Rules
- Supplementary materials deadline is the same as the paper deadline
- Reviewers are **not required** to review supplementary materials
- Supplementary materials must be **fully anonymous** (same as main paper)
- Any de-anonymizing content in supplementary materials triggers desk rejection

### What to Include
- Full proofs of theorems
- Additional experimental results and ablations
- Dataset documentation
- Implementation details
- Extended related work

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide)

## 13. Author Response/Rebuttal Period

### Timeline
- **Reviews released:** November 11, 2025 (9:00 PM UTC)
- **Public discussion period:** November 11 -- December 3, 2025
- **Author response deadline:** December 3, 2025 (9:00 PM UTC)
- **Private AC/reviewer discussion:** December 3, 2025 -- January 22, 2026
- **Decision notification:** January 25, 2026

### What Authors Can Do During Discussion
- **Revise the paper:** Title, abstract, and full content (including supplementary) can be changed
- **Post unlimited comments** as responses to individual reviews
- **Upload revised PDF** (PDFdiff is applied automatically to highlight changes)
- **Increase page limit to 10 pages** of main text
- **Change author order** (but not the set of authors)
- **Post multiple revisions** (reviewers not required to review all)

### Rules and Cautions
- Each comment has a word limit (exact limit set in OpenReview)
- Reviewers and ACs **may ignore significantly altered revisions** that go beyond addressing review feedback
- Clearly communicate all changes to reviewers and ACs
- If issues arise, contact Area Chair first; escalate to Program Chairs with subject prefix `[Authors-to-PCs]`

### Visibility
- Author responses are posted as anonymous comments
- Reviews are publicly visible and anonymous
- Public comments from non-participants require real names
- Visibility options: public, reviewers-and-up, ACs-and-up, or Program Chairs only

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide)

## 16. Ethics Review Process

### Code of Ethics
All participants must acknowledge and adhere to the [ICLR Code of Ethics](https://iclr.cc/public/CodeOfEthics), which has eight core principles:

1. Contribute to society and human well-being
2. Uphold high standards of scientific excellence
3. Avoid harm
4. Be honest, trustworthy, and transparent
5. Be fair and avoid discrimination
6. Respect creative work
7. Respect privacy
8. Honor confidentiality

### Ethics Statement (Optional but Recommended)
- Place at end of main text, before references
- Maximum 1 page; does not count toward page limit
- Address relevant issues: human subjects, dataset releases, harmful insights, bias/discrimination, privacy/security, legal compliance, research integrity, conflicts of interest

### Reviewer Ethics Reporting
- Reviewers complete a Code of Ethics (CoE) report with two questions:
  1. Is there a potential violation of the Code of Ethics?
  2. If yes, explain the potential violation
- Deadline for flagging ethics issues: November 26, 2025

### Escalation Path
- Authors or reviewers flag potential CoE violations via **private message to the Area Chair** on OpenReview
- Unresolved issues escalated to Program Chairs
- ICLR reserves the right to reject any work found to violate ethical guidelines **at any point**

**Source:** [ICLR Code of Ethics](https://iclr.cc/public/CodeOfEthics), [ICLR 2026 Reviewer Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/ReviewerGuide)

## 17. Conflict of Interest Rules

### OpenReview Profile Requirements
- All authors must have **current, accurate OpenReview profiles**
- Profiles must reflect institutional affiliations for at least the last 3 years
- Two types of conflicts must be declared:

### Domain Conflicts
- Declared via the **Career & Education History** section of OpenReview profile
- Reflect current and recent institutional affiliations
- Use judiciously -- only for genuine conflicts with the institution

### Personal Conflicts
- Declared via the **Advisors & Other Relations** section
- Cover: current/former advisors, advisees, close collaborators, co-authors within recent years
- Internship supervisors and hosts fall under personal conflicts (not domain)

### Author Restrictions
- No author additions after the **abstract deadline** (September 19, 2025)
- Author order can change until the paper submission deadline
- No author changes of any kind after the submission deadline
- Author set is frozen for camera-ready

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide)

## 18. OpenReview Specifics

ICLR pioneered the use of OpenReview and uses it differently from most venues.

### Key Differentiators
- **Open peer review:** All reviews and discussions are publicly visible (anonymous during review, deanonymized at decision)
- **Public comments:** Anyone with an OpenReview account can comment on submissions during the discussion period (must use real name)
- **Persistent record:** Submissions, reviews, and discussions remain permanently accessible
- **Withdrawn papers** remain visible in a "withdrawn papers" section (deanonymized immediately)

### Submission Process
1. Create/update OpenReview profile at https://openreview.net
2. All co-authors must have OpenReview accounts linked to their email
3. Submit abstract by September 19 via the submission form
4. Upload full paper PDF by September 24
5. Submissions cannot be edited between paper deadline and review release

### Submission Form Fields
- **Title** (editable until paper deadline; also during rebuttal)
- **Abstract** (mandatory at abstract deadline)
- **Authors** (locked after abstract deadline; order editable until paper deadline)
- **PDF** upload
- **Keywords**
- **TL;DR** (brief summary)
- **Primary area** (research area categorization)
- **Supplementary materials** upload
- **LLM usage disclosure**
- **Code of Ethics acknowledgment**

### Comment Visibility Options
Authors can set comment visibility to:
- Public (all logged-in users)
- Reviewers and above
- Area Chairs and above
- Program Chairs only

### Profile Management
- Link multiple email addresses to a single profile
- Profile merging available via info@openreview.net
- Author profile names appear as links for profile creation

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Author Guide](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide), [OpenReview ICLR 2026](https://openreview.net/group?id=ICLR.cc/2026/Conference)

## 19. Bibliography System

### Citation Package
- Uses **natbib** (included with the style files)
- Two citation commands:
  - `\citet{key}` -- narrative/textual: "Hinton et al. (2006) showed..."
  - `\citep{key}` -- parenthetical: "...as shown previously (Hinton et al., 2006)"
- For multi-author works with 3+ authors, use "et al." construct

### Bibliography Style
- Use the provided `iclr2026_conference.bst` file
- Any consistent reference format is acceptable, but the provided `.bst` is recommended
- References listed alphabetically by first author surname

### Citing Under-Review Papers
- ICLR provides **anonymous BibTeX entries** for papers under review at ICLR 2026
- Use these when citing concurrent ICLR submissions to preserve anonymity
- Available during the discussion phase

### Self-Citation
- Cite your own prior work in **third person**
- "Smith et al. (2024) previously demonstrated..." NOT "We previously demonstrated (Smith et al., 2024)..."

### Footnotes
- Number sequentially throughout the paper
- Place at bottom of the page where they appear
- Preceded by a 2-inch horizontal rule

**Source:** [ICLR 2026 Formatting Instructions](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=qioDi3afqm), [ICLR Master Template](https://github.com/ICLR/Master-Template)

## 20. Pre-Submission Checklist

Use this checklist before submitting to ICLR 2026.

### Profile and Registration
- [ ] All co-authors have current OpenReview profiles with accurate affiliations
- [ ] At least one author is registered as a reviewer for minimum 3 papers
- [ ] If any author is on 3+ submissions: that author registered to review minimum 6 papers
- [ ] Author list finalized (no changes possible after abstract deadline Sep 19)

### Anonymization
- [ ] No author names, affiliations, or identifying information in the PDF
- [ ] No author names in PDF metadata
- [ ] Acknowledgments removed or anonymized
- [ ] All self-citations written in third person
- [ ] All URLs are anonymous (no personal GitHub, lab websites, etc.)
- [ ] No visitor tracking on linked websites
- [ ] Video demos are fully anonymous
- [ ] Supplementary materials contain no identifying information

### Formatting
- [ ] Using official `iclr2026_conference.sty` with default (submission) option
- [ ] Paper compiles with US Letter paper size (not A4)
- [ ] Main text is 9 pages or fewer
- [ ] References are in a separate section after main text (unlimited pages)
- [ ] Appendix follows references (unlimited pages)
- [ ] Style file parameters not modified
- [ ] Title in 17-point small caps
- [ ] Body text in 10-point type
- [ ] Figures are legible with dark lines suitable for reproduction
- [ ] Pages are numbered

### Content
- [ ] Abstract is a single paragraph
- [ ] Claims are properly supported by evidence
- [ ] Prior work adequately cited and discussed
- [ ] Reproducibility statement included (recommended, after main text before references)
- [ ] Ethics statement included if paper raises ethical concerns (after main text before references)

### LLM Disclosure
- [ ] If LLMs were used significantly: disclosure section in appendix describing precise role
- [ ] LLM usage indicated in OpenReview submission form
- [ ] All LLM-generated content verified for accuracy (no hallucinated references)
- [ ] LLMs not listed as authors

### Dual Submission
- [ ] Paper not substantially similar to any published or under-review work
- [ ] Paper not submitted to another peer-reviewed venue concurrently
- [ ] If posted on arXiv: cited in third person in the submission

### Submission Form
- [ ] Abstract submitted by September 19, 2025
- [ ] Title finalized
- [ ] Keywords and TL;DR filled in
- [ ] Primary area selected
- [ ] PDF uploaded by September 24, 2025
- [ ] Supplementary materials uploaded (if any) by paper deadline
- [ ] Code of Ethics acknowledged

### Reciprocal Reviewing
- [ ] At least one qualified author (prior ICLR/NeurIPS/ICML/equivalent publication) registered as reviewer
- [ ] Registration completed before the deadline
- [ ] Understand that failure to complete reviews by rebuttal stage may result in desk rejection
