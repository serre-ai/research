# Section Guide: Conclusion

## Purpose
Summarize contributions and leave the reader with a clear takeaway. This is the last thing the reader remembers — make it count.

## Structure (target: 5% of main text, ~0.5 page)

### Sentence 1-2: Restate the thesis
Not a copy of the abstract — restate in light of what the paper showed. "We have shown that reasoning gaps are predictable..." not "In this paper, we presented..."

### Sentence 3-5: Enumerate contributions
Mirror the introduction's contribution list, but now with results:
- Before: "A six-type taxonomy with testable predictions"
- After: "A six-type taxonomy validated across twelve models and 176K instances"

### Sentence 6-7: The practical takeaway
One sentence a practitioner can act on: "Match the intervention to the gap type: CoT for depth/serial gaps, tools for algorithmic gaps, nothing for architectural gaps."

### Final sentence: Forward-looking
Not vague ("future work is needed") but directional: "The framework suggests that verification complexity, not task difficulty, determines when AI oversight is tractable — a prediction we plan to test empirically."

## Do
- Be definitive — you've earned it with the results
- Reference the strongest result (the number a reviewer will remember)
- End with something that makes the reader think

## Don't
- Don't introduce new information or results
- Don't caveat everything you just proved (save limitations for Discussion)
- Don't say "in this paper we" (you already said it in the intro and abstract)
- Don't exceed half a page — conclusions that ramble lose impact
